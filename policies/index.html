Housing policies and programs often focus on people or housing units as well as different income levels.

People or Housing Units

Programs that assist individuals or families try to increase their ability to obtain or maintain housing (i.e. increase their ability to “demand” housing in the market). Programs that focus on units try to maintain or increase the “supply” of affordable housing. Look for the icons to see the focus of each policy.

Income Elibibility

Housing programs use a tool called “Area Median Income” (AMI). Median income is the income level where 50% of people make more and 50% make less (think median = middle). Our area median includes Marin, San Francisco, and San Mateo counties.

Programs often define Income eligibility as a percent of AMI. So if the AMI is $100,000, 50% AMI is $50,000 and 150% is $150,000.
