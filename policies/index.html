Housing policies and programs can be constrained to individual or family income levels and are focused on either helping people increase access to housing or building and maintaining housing units. Those policy frames are represented below in the list of policies and programs.

Income Elibibility

Housing programs use a tool called “Area Median Income” (AMI). Median income is the income level where 50% of people make more and 50% make less (think median = middle). Our area median includes Marin, San Francisco, and San Mateo counties.

Programs often define Income eligibility as a percent of AMI. So, for example, if the AMI is $100,000, 50% AMI is $50,000 and 150% is $150,000. The AMI will vary by household size.

Look for the visual indicating the maximum AMI eligibility for each particular program or policy.

People or Housing Units

Programs that assist individuals or families try to increase their ability to obtain or maintain housing (i.e. increase their ability to “demand” housing in the market). Programs that focus on units try to maintain or increase the “supply” of affordable housing.
